Arduino Nano / UNO R3

ATmega328P microcontroller with built-in ADC. Processes sensor data and triggers the output.

Flex Sensors (×4)

Conductive ink strips whose resistance increases with bending. Mounted on each finger to capture bend angle (0–1023 range).

MPU-6050 Accelerometer

MEMS 3-axis gyroscope + accelerometer. Captures hand position in X, Y, Z axes via I2C communication.

HC-05 Bluetooth Module

Enables wireless transmission to a smartphone. The paired app converts text to speech with adjustable pitch and volume.

LCD Display

Shows the identified gesture as text for visual confirmation and additional accessibility.

Speaker / Speech IC

Outputs the audio equivalent of the recognized gesture — audible to anyone nearby without requiring any app.

How It Works

01

Wear & Power On

Glove with sensors, Arduino, and Bluetooth module is worn and switched on.

02

Perform Gesture

User makes a hand gesture — letter, number, or custom phrase from ISL / ASL.

03

Sensors Capture Values

4 flex sensors measure finger bending; MPU-6050 captures x, y, z orientation — all fed as analog signals to Arduino's ADC pins.

04

Arduino Pattern Matches

Digital values compared against predefined gesture database (C/C++ in Arduino IDE). On match, corresponding text is queued.

05

Bluetooth Transmission

Matched text sent via HC-05 to paired Android phone in real time.

06

Speech & Text Output

Android app displays text and speaks it aloud. Pitch, speed, and volume are customizable.
